Alison CSS Grid

A tool that aims to reconcile Mobiles, Old (IE 5.x) plus Modern Browsers & your Users
focused on Latest Enhancements in Web Design but with a Strong, Effective Base.

The actual webdesign fails with templates offers (paid & free) which don't an­swer the real si­tua­tions of final users (poor surfing 2G Smart­Phone band­width in the streets if you're too far from cellular network antena) with huge ja­va­script effects on too big images and several me­ga­bytes weight by page ;( Ouch!

This tool is made with speed in mind and ca­pa­bi­li­ties with a large brow­sers mar­ket. HTML5 & Mo­bile first. For real world wi­thout huge ja­va­script li­brary. Be­cause we need a simple solution that works fine across all plate­forms (that's sure for in­ter­na­tional e-co­mmerce pro­jects: who re­ally wants to lose cus­to­mers with bro­ken web­sites elements on current 28.98% Win XP mar­ket?)

Alison isn't a tren­dly hyp­ster frame­work: just few lines of mi­ni­mal code, called "RWD CSS com­po­nents mo­dules". Is it worth to say it works per­fec­tly with the dy­no­saur Inter­net Ex­plo­rer © 5.1 6 (and ine­vi­ta­bly with all other upper ver­sions)?

Alison is just a real tool for a real world.

Bored with other com­plex so­lu­tions? Here a co­lumn is na­med .col, sized sub blocks fo­llow a sim­ple con­ven­tion: a "c" le­tter and num­ber (percent width). Pre­tty sim­ple wi­thout the head­ache. All class attri­butes are short in order to pre­ser­ve your page's wei­ght. Tes­ted with Google ® Page Speed and a uh oh 100% re­sult challenge. Free of charges, under MIT li­cense. Damned, Dude!
